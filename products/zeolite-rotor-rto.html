Overview

Built for large-volume VOC exhaust with lower concentration.

The Zeolite Rotor + RTO system concentrates low-concentration VOCs from a large exhaust volume into a smaller, higher-concentration stream before thermal oxidation. It helps reduce the size and load of the RTO while keeping the overall system suitable for continuous industrial operation.

01

VOC Concentration

Zeolite adsorption concentrates VOCs before final oxidation.

02

Reduced RTO Load

Concentration reduces treated air volume entering the RTO section.

03

Continuous Operation

Adsorption, desorption and oxidation are matched as one process route.

04

System Integration

Can be combined with scrubbers, filters, ESP and polishing systems.

Process Flow

How the Zeolite Rotor + RTO system works.

1

Pre-treatment

Dust, sticky mist or moisture are reduced to protect the RTO core.

2

Adsorption

Large-volume exhaust passes through the zeolite rotor adsorption zone.

3

Desorption

A smaller hot air stream desorbs VOCs from the rotor into a concentrated stream.

4

Reduced RTO Load

Clean hot gas transfers heat back to the ceramic bed for reuse.

5

Discharge

Treated gas is discharged after meeting the designed emission target.

Technical Reference

Typical selection parameters for rotor + RTO.

Applicable PollutantsLarge-volume, low-concentration VOC exhaust from coating, printing, new materials, plastic processing, chemical and other continuous production processes.
Common ConfigurationPre-filter / scrubber / ESP + zeolite rotor concentrator + desorption system + RTO main unit + fan + stack + control cabinet.
Selection FactorsAir volume, VOC concentration, exhaust temperature, humidity, dust content, solvent type, operating hours and emission target.
Integration OptionsDry filtration, wet scrubbing, ESP, activated carbon polishing and heat recovery options can be combined when required.
Engineering OutputConcentration ratio, airflow design, equipment layout, duct connection, control logic and installation guidance.
